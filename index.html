Brownian Motion Simulator

About this app

This simulator demonstrates Brownian motion, a fundamental stochastic process in physics and mathematics. Brownian motion models the random movement of particles suspended in a fluid, and it provides the building block for many advanced concepts in statistical mechanics, probability theory and financial mathematics. Mathematically, the increments of a Brownian path over disjoint time intervals are independent and normally distributed with mean 0 and variance equal to the length of the interval. This property makes Brownian motion the continuous‑time analogue of a random walk and leads to the diffusion equation and Wiener process that underpin stochastic calculus.

To explore, choose a number of simulation steps and a time increment Δt below, then click Simulate. The app will draw a two‑dimensional Brownian path starting from the centre of the canvas and report basic statistics such as the final displacement and mean squared displacement. By adjusting Δt and the number of steps, you can observe how the path becomes rougher and more widely spread as total time increases. This experiment highlights the fundamental result that the mean displacement tends to zero while the variance grows linearly with elapsed time.
